### Short Notes: Struggle, Deep Thinking, and Math

#### Struggle Helps Learning

* Open-ended and challenging math problems help the brain grow.
* A **growth mindset** helps students see challenges as opportunities to learn.
* The best learners make mistakes, learn from them, and keep trying.

#### Speed vs. Understanding

* Being **good at math does not mean being fast**.
* Deep thinking is more important than quick answers.
* Understanding concepts fully leads to better learning.

#### Example: Laurent Schwartz

* Won the **Fields Medal**, the highest award in mathematics.
* Was one of the slowest students in math at school.
* Believed intelligence comes from **deep understanding**, not speed.
* Took time to understand ideas and connections.

#### Deep Mathematical Thinking

* Ask questions like:

  * Why does this work?
  * How is this connected to other methods?
  * What would a diagram of this look like?
* Focus on understanding relationships between ideas, not memorizing steps.

#### Key Message

* Struggling and making mistakes help the brain grow.
* Deep understanding is more valuable than working quickly.
* Success in math comes from persistence, curiosity, and careful thinking.

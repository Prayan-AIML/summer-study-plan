# **United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)**

## Background

* Children have always had **human rights**, but for many years they were mainly protected through **charity**, not legal rights.
* After **World War I**, **Eglantine Jebb** campaigned for the **First Declaration of the Rights of the Child (1924)**.
* The **UN adopted an expanded Declaration in 1959**.
* In **1979 (International Year of the Child)**, **Poland proposed** creating a convention specifically for children's rights.
* After **10 years of discussion**, the **UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (UNCRC)** was adopted in **1989**.
* It is now the **most widely ratified human rights treaty** in the world.

---

# United Nations (UN)

* International organisation that promotes:

  * Peace and security
  * Human rights
* Human rights are based on **3 principles:**

  * **Dignity**
  * **Equality**
  * **Respect**

---

# What is the UNCRC?

* International treaty protecting **everyone under 18** (a child).
* Sets out the rights that every child is entitled to.
* Signed and ratified by **196 countries** (all UN members except the USA).

### Who Has Duties Under the Convention?

**States (governments)** are the main duty-bearers. They must:

* Respect children's rights.
* Protect children's rights.
* Fulfil children's rights.

Children's rights apply regardless of:

* Race or ethnicity
* Gender
* Language
* Disability
* Religion or beliefs
* Family status
* Nationality or citizenship

### Parents

* Also have rights under the Convention.
* Entitled to:

  * Government support in raising children.
  * Guide and advise their children.

---

# Responsibilities

* Individual citizens are **not legally responsible** for enforcing the Convention.
* However, if they work for the government (e.g. teachers), they must uphold children's rights.
* Example:

  * Schools must stop bullying so every child can learn safely.

---

# Four General Principles of the UNCRC

These apply to **every article** in the Convention.

1. **Non-discrimination**

   * Every child has equal rights.

2. **Best interests of the child**

   * Children's wellbeing must come first in all decisions.

3. **Right to life, survival and development**

   * Every child has the right to grow, develop and survive.

4. **Respect for children's views**

   * Children's opinions should be listened to and considered.

---

# Five Categories of Rights

### 1. Civil Rights & Freedoms
Rights to identity, nationality, freedom of expression, thought, and protection from harm.

### 2. Family Environment & Alternative Care
Rights to parental care, protection from separation, and alternative care when needed.

### 3. Basic Health & Welfare
Rights to healthcare, nutrition, clean water, and an adequate standard of living.

### 4. Education, Leisure & Cultural Activities
Rights to education, play, rest, and participation in culture.

### 5. Special Protection Measures
Rights for children in especially vulnerable situations — refugees, children in conflict, those exploited or abused.

---

# Rights are Interdependent

* All rights depend on each other.
* If one right is not protected, others are affected.
* Example:

  * Poor living conditions → poor health → difficulty learning.

---

# Three Optional Protocols

1. Children in **armed conflict**.
2. **Sale of children, child prostitution and child pornography.**
3. **Complaints procedure** allowing children to report rights violations.

---

# Monitoring the Convention

* Done by the **UN Committee on the Rights of the Child**.
* Countries submit a **report every 5 years**.
* Information also comes from:

  * NGOs
  * Children's Commissioners
  * Children themselves.
* Committee publishes **Concluding Observations**:

  * Reviews progress.
  * Gives recommendations for improvement.

---

# How Countries Should Implement the UNCRC

States should:

* Include children's rights in national laws.
* Create a **National Plan for Children**.
* Monitor government spending on children.
* Use reliable data about children's lives.
* Raise awareness of the Convention among adults and children.

---

# Why the UNCRC Matters

* Recognises children as **rights holders**, not just people needing charity.
* Governments become **accountable** for protecting children's rights.
* Children's organisations and children themselves can demand change using the Convention.
* Ensures every child is treated with **equality, dignity and respect**.
* Goal: **Every child, everywhere, should enjoy the rights in the UNCRC.**

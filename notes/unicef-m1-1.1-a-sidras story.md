# **Sidra's Story – Life in the Zaatari Refugee Camp (Jordan)**

* **Sidra** is a **12-year-old girl from Syria**, studying in **Grade 5**.
* She has lived in the **Zaatari refugee camp in Jordan** for **1½ years** after her family fled the war in Syria.
* The journey to Jordan was difficult, involving **days of crossing the desert**.
* Before leaving Syria, Sidra's **kite got stuck in a tree**, and she still wonders if it is there. This shows how much she misses home.

### Family Life

* Sidra has a **large family**, including **three brothers**, one of whom is a baby.
* She jokes that she was a **stronger baby** because she cried less than her younger brother.
* Her mother makes sure the whole family **eats dinner together**, helping them stay united despite hardships.

### Education in the Camp

* School is very important to Sidra.
* Students take care of their books and bags, treating it like a game to keep them clean and undamaged.
* Sidra does her homework regularly and likes answering questions in class.
* Some children refuse to attend school because they are waiting to return to Syria, but Sidra thinks this is a mistake because education should not stop.

### Daily Life in Zaatari

* The smell of fresh bread from a nearby bakery fills the walk to school.
* Boys often spend time on computers, though girls are usually not allowed to join them.
* Many men exercise in the gym, saying they want to be strong for the journey home, though Sidra thinks they also enjoy looking strong.
* Younger boys often wrestle and play fighting games.

### Sports and Recreation

* Football is very popular in the camp.
* There are so many children that games must be played quickly so everyone gets a turn.
* Unlike in her hometown, **girls in Zaatari are allowed to play football**, which makes Sidra very happy.

### Sidra's Feelings and Hopes

* Although life in the camp is safer, Sidra deeply misses **Syria and her old home**.
* She believes she will not stay in the camp forever.
* Her teacher says the clouds above Jordan also came from Syria.
* This gives Sidra hope that **one day she, like the clouds, will return home**.

### Main Themes

* **Hope and resilience**
* **Importance of education**
* **Family support and unity**
* **Life as a refugee**
* **Dream of returning home**
* **Children adapting to difficult circumstances**

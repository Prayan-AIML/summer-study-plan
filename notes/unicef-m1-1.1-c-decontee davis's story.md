# **Decontee Davis' Story – Surviving Ebola in Liberia**

* The speaker is **Decontee Davis**, a survivor of the **Ebola** outbreak in **Liberia**.
* Her story is told as a prayer, expressing gratitude, grief, faith, and hope.
* She thanks God for helping Liberia recover and for giving her the strength to continue helping others.

### Life During the Ebola Outbreak

* When Decontee became ill, she was very frightened.
* She feared going to the hospital because many people believed entering treatment centres meant death.
* She eventually received treatment and survived.
* While in the treatment ward, she witnessed many people dying around her.

### Loss of Her Husband

* The man she loved became sick as well.
* He had to wait several days longer than she did before receiving treatment.
* She could hear him calling out, but she never saw him again.
* Her husband died from Ebola, leaving her to cope with enormous grief.

### Recovery and New Purpose

* Decontee describes surviving Ebola as being "born again."
* She compares herself to a phoenix rising from the ashes.
* After recovering, she dedicated herself to helping others affected by the disease.
* She comforts and cares for sick people when others are afraid to do so.

### Stigma Faced by Survivors

* Many people feared Ebola survivors, wrongly believing they could still spread the disease.
* People were often afraid to talk to or interact with survivors.
* Her own son was excluded from playing with other children.
* She works to educate people that Ebola survivors are safe and should not be feared.

### Helping Orphans and Communities

* Ebola left many children without parents.
* Decontee works with orphans and families affected by the outbreak.
* She believes survivors have a responsibility to support those who are suffering.
* Her goal is to provide compassion, comfort, and hope.

### Liberia's Recovery

* Decontee notes signs that Liberia is healing:

  * Markets are reopening.
  * Children are returning to school.
  * Daily life is gradually returning to normal.
* Despite improvements, she recognizes that communities remain vulnerable and need continued support.

### Main Themes

* **Survival and resilience**
* **Faith and hope**
* **Effects of disease outbreaks**
* **Loss and grief**
* **Stigma and discrimination**
* **Community support**
* **Recovery and rebuilding after crisis**

### Key Message

Even after experiencing illness, loss, and rejection, Decontee chooses to help others. Her story shows how courage, compassion, and faith can help individuals and communities recover from tragedy.

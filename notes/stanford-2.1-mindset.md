### Short Notes: Growth Mindset vs Fixed Mindset

#### Mindsets

* People have different beliefs about learning, called **mindsets**.
* **Growth Mindset:** Intelligence can improve through effort and practice.
* **Fixed Mindset:** Intelligence is fixed and cannot change much.

#### Growth Mindset

* Believes hard work leads to improvement.
* Keeps trying even after mistakes.
* Sees challenges as opportunities to learn.
* Does not give up when work is difficult.

#### Fixed Mindset

* Believes people are either naturally smart or not.
* May avoid challenges.
* Often gives up after mistakes or failure.
* Thinks difficulty means a lack of ability.

#### Effects on Learning

* Students with a growth mindset generally achieve higher results.
* Their positive beliefs help them work harder and longer.
* They learn from mistakes instead of being discouraged by them.

#### Helpful Behaviors for Math Learning

* Persistence when work is difficult.
* Learning from mistakes.
* Thinking creatively to solve problems.
* Believing that improvement is possible through effort.

#### Key Message

* Success in math depends not only on ability but also on mindset.
* A growth mindset helps people learn, improve, and achieve more.

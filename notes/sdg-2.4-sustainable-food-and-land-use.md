## Sustainable Food and Land Use (SDG 2.4)

The **fourth of the six transformations**: shifting to sustainable food systems and land use. Agriculture is probably the **most important human driver of environmental degradation** — it contributes to all three great environmental crises simultaneously.

### How Agriculture Damages the Environment

* **Climate change** — energy inputs emit CO₂; fertilisers emit **nitrous oxide**; livestock (cows) and rice paddies emit **methane**.
* **Biodiversity loss** — forests cleared for farms, pasture, logging, cattle ranches, and palm oil plantations (Amazon, Congo Basin, Indonesian rainforests).
* **Pollution** — biomass burning adds particulate matter; pesticides; nitrogen/phosphorus fertilisers run into rivers → reach estuaries → **eutrophication → dead zones**.
* **Water** — agriculture uses ~**70% of all fresh water**, often by pumping groundwater faster than it recharges (unsustainable).
* **Health** — processed food directly drives obesity, metabolic disorders, cardiovascular disease.
* **Self-threat** — climate change (caused partly by agriculture) destroys crops via intense storms, flooding, and sea-level rise.

**Relevant SDGs:** SDG 2 (food), SDG 6 (fresh water), SDG 14 (marine ecosystems), SDG 15 (terrestrial ecosystems).

---

### The 5 Pillars of Transformation

**Pillar 1 — Sustainable food systems**
* Higher-yield crop varieties → no need to clear forests for more land.
* Reduce post-harvest losses (low-income countries lose 20–30% of food before it reaches market).
* Restore degraded lands and **reforest** previously cleared areas to recapture CO₂.

**Pillar 2 — Protected areas (draw hard boundaries)**
* Identify and legally protect high-biodiversity zones from agricultural encroachment.
* E.O. Wilson (Harvard biologist): protect **half the Earth** to stop human-caused biodiversity collapse.
* Includes both **terrestrial** protected areas and **marine** protected areas (coral reefs, fisheries).
* Fisheries: use **tradable permits and quotas** to prevent overfishing and stock collapse.

**Pillar 3 — Shift diets**
* Heavy beef consumption is extremely land-intensive: **10–15 kg of feed grain per 1 kg of beef**.
* Reducing meat consumption → healthier diets + lower environmental footprint.

**Pillar 4 — Govern global supply chains**
* Food is traded globally — e.g. China imports soybeans from Brazil → Amazon cleared for soy farms; imports tropical hardwood from Malaysia → Malaysian rainforests destroyed.
* Right now: **nobody is accountable** for the environmental damage in supply chains.
* Policy answer: **the entire supply chain must be responsible** — producers, traders, and consumers.

**Pillar 5 — Land use planning**
* Any parcel of land can serve multiple purposes: farming, cities, natural resources, ecosystem services, carbon storage, biodiversity habitat.
* Currently countries don't plan these trade-offs clearly.
* Need **systematic land-use planning** that sets priorities, draws limits, and gives incentives — deciding deliberately: grow food here, protect habitat there, store carbon here.

---

### Key Message

Agriculture is both a major **cause** of environmental crisis and a major **victim** of it. Fixing food and land use requires sustainable farming, hard biodiversity boundaries, diet shifts, supply chain accountability, and serious land-use planning — all at once.

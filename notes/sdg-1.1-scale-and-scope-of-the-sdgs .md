## Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

**Jeffrey Sachs**, a professor at **Columbia University**, serves as Special Advisor to **António Guterres** on the SDGs. He also leads the **United Nations Sustainable Development Solutions Network**, a network of over 700 universities and research organizations working to help achieve the Sustainable Development Goals.

The SDGs are a set of **17 global goals** adopted by all **193 UN member states** in 2015. They provide a framework for global cooperation and development from **2015–2030**. The goals were created because the world faces serious challenges such as poverty, hunger, inequality, climate change, and environmental degradation.

### Purpose of the SDGs

The SDGs aim to shift the world onto a path of **sustainable development**, where:

* Prosperity is shared by everyone.
* Societies are inclusive and fair.
* The environment is protected.
* Future generations can meet their own needs.

Countries recognized that while modern technology can solve many problems, current economic growth is also causing environmental damage and increasing inequality.

### Important SDGs

Many goals focus on ending deprivation:

* **SDG 1:** End extreme poverty.
* **SDG 2:** End hunger.
* **SDG 3:** Good health and well-being.
* **SDG 4:** Quality education for all.

Other goals focus on equality and environmental protection:

* **SDG 5:** Gender equality.
* **SDG 10:** Reduced inequalities.
* **SDG 13:** Climate action.
* **SDG 14:** Protect oceans and marine life.
* **SDG 15:** Protect land ecosystems.
* **SDG 17:** Global partnerships to achieve all goals.

### Agenda 2030

The SDGs are part of **"Transforming the World: The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development."** It is described as a plan of action for:

* **People** – improving well-being and social inclusion.
* **Planet** – protecting the environment.
* **Prosperity** – ensuring economic growth benefits everyone.

A key message of Agenda 2030 is that **eradicating poverty is the greatest global challenge** and is essential for sustainable development.

### Leave No One Behind

The member states pledged that development should benefit everyone. This principle, known as **"Leave No One Behind,"** means that no person, community, or country should be excluded from progress. Special attention should be given to vulnerable and disadvantaged groups.

### Achieving the SDGs

Achieving the goals will require major changes in society, including:

* Cleaner energy and sustainable industries.
* Better healthcare and education systems.
* Reduced inequality and discrimination.
* Protection of ecosystems and biodiversity.
* Strong international cooperation.

The SDG framework includes:

* **17 Goals**
* **169 Targets**
* **230 Indicators** used to measure progress.

Achieving these goals requires cooperation among governments, businesses, civil society, universities, and citizens around the world.

# **Module 1.4 – UNICEF in the Field**

## Activity 1 – Children's Rights Threatened During Emergencies

Emergencies (war, conflict, disasters, disease outbreaks) can threaten many rights under the **Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC).** UNICEF works to protect these rights.

### Rights that may be threatened

* **Article 2** – No discrimination (religion, beliefs, family status, ethnicity).
* **Article 3** – Inadequate care and protection if services are destroyed or children are displaced.
* **Article 7** – Birth registration may be lost; children may become separated from parents.
* **Article 8** – Loss of identity after separation from family.
* **Article 9** – Separation from parents during conflict or imprisonment.
* **Articles 11 & 35** – Increased risk of child trafficking.
* **Article 12** – Children may not be listened to when decisions are made.
* **Articles 13 & 14** – Restrictions on freedom of expression and religion.
* **Article 18** – Parents may be unable to care for children.
* **Article 19** – Increased risk of violence, abuse, and neglect.
* **Article 22** – Refugee children may lose protection.
* **Article 24** – Access to healthcare may be lost.
* **Article 26** – Social security systems may collapse.
* **Article 27** – Adequate standard of living may not be maintained.
* **Article 28** – Schools may be destroyed; education disrupted.
* **Article 30** – Minority children may lose their culture and language.
* **Article 31** – Less opportunity for play and recreation.
* **Article 32** – Child labour and economic exploitation.
* **Article 34** – Sexual abuse and exploitation.
* **Articles 37 & 40** – Unlawful imprisonment or lack of legal protection.
* **Article 38** – Protection of children during armed conflict may not be respected.

### UNICEF's Role

* Protect children's rights during emergencies.
* Provide healthcare, education, child protection, nutrition and humanitarian assistance.
* Ensure children's rights are respected throughout the response.

# **Activity 2 – Using the Core Commitments for Children (CCC)**

The **CCC (Core Commitments for Children)** guides UNICEF on what actions to take when children's rights are threatened.

### Example 1 – Discrimination (Article 2)

Relevant CCC areas:

* Rapid assessment, monitoring and evaluation.
* Child Protection Strategic Results:

  * **Commitment 2:** Monitor and report serious child protection violations and ensure accountability.

### Example 2 – Healthcare (Article 24)

* All health commitments work together to ensure children receive preventive and curative healthcare equally.

### Example 3 – Education (Article 28)

* All education commitments aim to ensure children continue learning during emergencies.
* UNICEF helps restore safe access to education.

---

# **Key Takeaways**

* The **CRC identifies children's rights**.
* The **CCC explains how UNICEF protects those rights** during humanitarian emergencies.
* Every commitment includes actions for **Preparedness**, **Response**, and **Early Recovery**.
* UNICEF staff should know how the CCC is organised so they can quickly find the right guidance during emergencies.

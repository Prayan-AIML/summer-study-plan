## Differential Diagnosis (SDG 1.4)

Think like a **policymaker**: look at your country, city, or region and ask — *what do we need to do here to achieve the SDGs?* Every place needs the **six transformations** (education, health, energy, land use, cities, digital), but the **priorities differ** from place to place. Good policy means understanding the general principles **and** the specific context — an idea borrowed from medicine called **differential diagnosis** (or "clinical economics").

### Why Priorities Differ

* **Level of development** – a rich country may already have healthcare & education but be a big greenhouse-gas emitter; a poor country may still need basic health and schooling.
* **Inequality & social division** – divided by income, region, race, or religion poses extra challenges.
* **Physical geography** – coastal (trade benefits, but sea-level risk) vs landlocked (costly trade, no sea-level risk); tropical (malaria, dengue, Zika) vs temperate (cold winters, but longer growing seasons with warming).

### The Diagnostic Tool: the SDG Index

* Published each year by the **UN Sustainable Development Solutions Network**.
* Built on **17 sub-indexes** (one per SDG), covering about **160 countries**.
* Scored **0 (worst) to 100 (achieved)**, shown as a **traffic-light dashboard**:

  * **Red** – far from achieving the SDG.
  * **Green** – on track.
  * **Yellow / beige** – somewhere in between.

### Case 1: United States — *rich but not sustainable*

* Ranks **9th** in income, but only **42nd of 157** on the SDGs (2017).
* Dashboard has **no greens** — only yellows, beiges, and reds.
* Problems: high inequality, social exclusion, gender discrimination, and a poor, worsening environment.
* **Priority:** more **fairness** and **environmental sustainability**.

### Case 2: Burkina Faso — *poor, lacks prosperity*

* Ranks about **140th** in income (near the bottom).
* **Red** on poverty, **health (SDG 3)**, and **education (SDG 4)** — extreme poverty, child stunting/undernourishment, no basic healthcare, low secondary-school completion.
* But the **environment scores better** (yellow/green on SDGs 13, 14, 15) because a poor economy pollutes and emits far less.
* **Priority:** basic investments in **health, education, and core infrastructure** (electrification, roads, transport, broadband).

### Key Message

Being **rich is not the same as being sustainable**, and poor countries need basic development first. Each country must make its own **differential diagnosis** of which SDGs matter most — and keep updating it on the path to **2030**.

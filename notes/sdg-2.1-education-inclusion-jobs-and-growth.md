## Transformation 1: Education, Inclusion, Jobs and Growth

To achieve the SDGs and the **Paris Climate Agreement**, society must undergo major transformations. A **transformation** means making long-term investments in infrastructure, research, education, healthcare, and people's skills. These changes require cooperation between governments, businesses, and civil society, as well as significant financial investment.

### 1. Quality Education for All

The first priority is ensuring that **every child receives quality education**. Education should begin in **early childhood (ages 3–5)** because this is a crucial stage of brain development and learning. **SDG 4** calls for universal access to education from pre-primary to secondary school.

This is especially important in low-income countries, where many children do not complete secondary school. Completing high school is now essential because modern jobs require digital and technical skills, while many traditional manual jobs are disappearing due to automation and technology.

**Key points:**

* Universal access from pre-primary to secondary education.
* Focus on both **access and quality**.
* Prepare students for a technology-driven economy.
* No child should be left behind.

---

### 2. Gender Equality in Education and Employment

In many countries, girls are kept home to do unpaid household work instead of earning wages.

To achieve **SDG 5 (Gender Equality)**, countries need to:

* Ensure equal education for girls and boys.
* Protect women's legal rights at work.
* Increase women's participation in the paid workforce.
* Eliminate discrimination in employment.

---

### 3. From School to Work

Finishing school does not always guarantee a job. Many students leave school with qualifications but lack the practical skills employers need.

One successful example is **Germany**, which has a strong **apprenticeship system**. Students receive vocational training while working in companies, helping them gain practical experience and reducing youth unemployment.

A successful school-to-work transition should:

* Develop practical workplace skills.
* Provide vocational education and apprenticeships.
* Reduce youth unemployment.
* Match education with industry needs.

---

### 4. Fair Labour Standards

The SDGs promote **decent work** and protection of workers' rights. The **International Labour Organization (ILO)** sets global standards for fair employment, including safe working conditions, fair wages, and the elimination of child labour.

---

### 5. Innovation and Economic Growth

Innovation is another key part of this transformation. Countries need to invest in:

* Research and development (R&D).
* Science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).
* Universities and research institutions.
* New technologies and inventions.

Many developing countries currently rely on technologies created elsewhere. While importing technology helps development, countries that create their own innovations grow faster and benefit economically through new inventions and patents.

**SDG 9** encourages countries to build scientific knowledge and innovation systems so that all nations can participate in technological progress.

### Summary of Transformation 1

This transformation combines:

* Quality education for all.
* Gender equality.
* Smooth transition from school to employment.
* Fair labour rights.
* Innovation and technological development.

Together, these create **inclusive economic growth**, skilled workers, and better opportunities for everyone.
